# Venture Math 9+ Roadmap

## Objective

Move Venture Math Lab from its current estimated `7.4 / 10` range to a product that credibly deserves a `9+ / 10` institutional score from demanding founders, investors, operators, and educators.

This document is intentionally strict. A `9+` product is not one with many features. It is one that is hard to complain about.

## Reality Check

### Current estimated score

- Overall: `7.4 / 10`
- Founder and operator usefulness: `7.8 / 10`
- Investor and venture realism: `7.1 / 10`
- Institutional and boardroom polish: `6.8 / 10`

### What a 9+ means here

To earn `9+`, the app must be:

- mathematically hard to fault in standard use cases
- financially credible to experienced venture users
- visually refined enough to feel premium
- operationally reliable across layouts, exports, and devices
- pedagogically excellent without becoming simplistic
- shareable in boardrooms, classrooms, memos, and investor discussions

### What a 10 means here

A `10 / 10` is not a realistic near-term goal. It would require:

- legal-grade term modeling
- outstanding design polish
- nearly no trust leaks
- exceptional pedagogy
- repeatable praise from elite users across all target audiences

The right target is:

- `9.0 to 9.2`: elite and highly recommendable
- `9.3 to 9.5`: category-leading
- `10`: effectively complaint-proof in its intended scope

## Score Ladder

### To reach 8.0

Required:

- no obvious UI overlaps or layout failures
- no raw money-entry confusion on major forms
- no ambiguous labels on key outputs
- stable report, compare, calculator, simulator, cap-table, fund, and operator pages

### To reach 8.5

Required:

- stronger report quality
- clear assumptions and limitations on exported outputs
- tighter chart captions and legends
- consistent support-status signaling (`standard`, `approximate`, `unsupported`)
- benchmark-backed defaults surfaced in-app

### To reach 9.0

Required:

- strong trust across all three engines
- no serious venture-naive shortcuts in common use cases
- board-grade outputs
- highly polished responsive UI
- classroom-quality explainability
- much stronger QA coverage

### To reach 9.5

Required:

- premium design system execution
- differentiated pedagogy
- highly credible benchmark layer
- excellent compare and narrative surfaces
- complaint resistance from sophisticated daily users

## The Six Workstreams That Actually Matter

## 1. Trust and Correctness

### Goal

Remove the remaining reasons a sophisticated user would say, "I do not trust this output."

### Required work

- enforce a single canonical capital semantics model everywhere
- eliminate remaining ambiguous or duplicative inputs
- add stronger validation and prevent silent bad states
- review deterministic, Monte Carlo, and waterfall outputs for label accuracy
- tighten unsupported-term handling

### Done means

- no known contradictory input flows remain
- every major metric traces cleanly to assumptions
- unsupported scenarios are blocked or visibly downgraded

## 2. Venture Realism

### Goal

Make the app feel credible to serious seed and Series A users, not just educational users.

### Required work

- deepen SAFE and note realism where behavior is still simplified
- refine anti-dilution treatment where current approximations are too coarse
- improve multi-series preferred logic and threshold visualization
- strengthen reserve, pro rata, and dilution attribution narratives
- make benchmark assumptions citeable and visible

### Done means

- an experienced VC no longer immediately spots standard scenario shortcuts that undermine confidence

## 3. Boardroom Output

### Goal

Make the app export surfaces good enough to paste into a memo, class packet, or board pre-read.

### Required work

- upgrade `/report` into a true memo surface
- improve compare mode to support executive summaries
- add better print styling
- tighten CSV and markdown export structure
- add chart subtitles, assumptions blocks, and support labels to exports

### Done means

- exported output looks intentional, labeled, and credible outside the app

## 4. Pedagogy

### Goal

Move from "helpful" to "teaches extremely well."

### Required work

- create a true Learn mode behavior across all engines
- add "what changed and why" explanations when users change assumptions
- clarify mean vs median vs percentile interpretation in more places
- explain where outputs are deterministic versus simulated versus term-driven
- improve glossary and methodology sequencing

### Done means

- a smart MBA student or founder becomes noticeably more competent after using the app

## 5. Design and Product Polish

### Goal

Make the product feel premium, composed, and hard to ridicule.

### Required work

- full responsive audit across all pages
- remove all remaining card density and spacing regressions
- unify metric card behavior and breakpoint strategy
- improve hierarchy, typography rhythm, and chart framing
- make empty, loading, and export states feel intentional

### Done means

- screenshots from the app look presentation-ready without apology

## 6. QA, Accessibility, and Regression Discipline

### Goal

Stop quality regressions from dragging the product back down.

### Required work

- add golden scenario tests across deterministic, simulation, and waterfall outputs
- add layout regression checks for key pages and breakpoints
- add broader invariants for ownership conservation, payout conservation, and scenario consistency
- improve keyboard and focus behavior
- add chart descriptions and color-independence checks

### Done means

- the app can evolve without reintroducing trust or layout failures

## Hard Gates for a 9+

These are non-negotiable.

1. No known layout overlap on primary pages.
2. No major label or metric mismatches.
3. Money entry must be clear and low-friction everywhere important.
4. Reports must be board-grade enough to share externally.
5. Benchmarks must be visible and properly framed as benchmark versus assumption.
6. Support-status boundaries must be obvious.
7. Responsive behavior must feel composed on desktop, tablet, and mobile.
8. A fresh institutional review must produce no major trust failures.

## What Will Keep the App Below 9

If any of these remain, the app should not be called 9+.

- recurring UI breakage
- report/export surfaces that feel amateur
- unexplained approximations in important venture cases
- ambiguous ownership or valuation semantics
- charts that look polished but teach the wrong lesson
- weak pedagogy for non-experts
- lack of responsive dignity on common screen widths

## Immediate Recommendation

The correct next move is not breadth. It is institutional finishing.

Priority order:

1. polish and stability across all existing surfaces
2. report and compare upgrades
3. stronger benchmark and explanation layer
4. rerun the institutional audit
5. use the new audit as the final climb to 9+

## Bottom Line

The app is already beyond toy quality. It has enough substance to justify a serious climb.

But `9+` will only happen if the product becomes:

- cleaner
- stricter
- more legible
- more shareable
- more benchmark-aware
- more difficult for sophisticated users to criticize

That is now an execution problem, not a vision problem.
