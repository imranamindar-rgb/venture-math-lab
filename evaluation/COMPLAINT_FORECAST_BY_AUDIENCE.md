# Complaint Forecast By Audience

## What Sophisticated Users Will Complain About After 30 Days

This forecast assumes serious repeated use, not a one-time demo.

## Founders

### What they will like

- The app makes dilution and venture math less opaque.

### What would make it excellent for them

- cleaner term-sheet comparisons
- clearer action implications
- stronger cash/runway overlays

### What would make it world class for them

- a tool they trust before negotiating a round

### Likely complaints

- “Why are there four different money fields?”
- “Can I trust the SAFE output if exits before conversion are handled this way?”
- “This helps me understand dilution, but it still does not tell me what to do.”
- “The output is not polished enough to send to my cofounders or board.”

## Seed Investors

### What they will like

- Entry valuation sensitivity and return-the-fund framing.

### What would make it excellent for them

- more faithful SAFE and note behavior
- better reserve analysis

### What would make it world class for them

- a seed-fund portfolio and reserves lab

### Likely complaints

- “This is still too company-level.”
- “I cannot trust the security-term treatment enough.”
- “The compare mode is too light.”

## Multistage Investors

### What they will like

- Some scenario framing value.

### What would make it excellent for them

- per-series stack logic
- better follow-on modeling

### What would make it world class for them

- true portfolio construction plus exit stack realism

### Likely complaints

- “The preferred stack is too compressed to be meaningful.”
- “There is no serious multistage fund lens here.”
- “This is fine for intuition, not for actual work.”

## Professors

### What they will like

- The product can make students engage with venture mechanics.

### What would make it excellent for them

- stronger learn mode
- cleaner assumptions status

### What would make it world class for them

- assignable, defensible, classroom-ready modules

### Likely complaints

- “I still have to explain around several outputs.”
- “The unsupported cases are exactly what students ask next.”
- “It teaches some things well but not rigorously enough across the board.”

## Students

### What they will like

- The product is easier to use than spreadsheets and more concrete than lectures.

### What would make it excellent for them

- guided examples
- interpretation coaching

### What would make it world class for them

- a product that turns confusion into durable intuition

### Likely complaints

- “I understand the screen, but I’m not always sure what result matters.”
- “Some terms are still intimidating.”
- “I can get outputs, but I’m not sure what conclusion to draw.”

## Operators

### What they will like

- Option pool and employee equity views.

### What would make it excellent for them

- stronger cash, burn, and operating-constraints context

### What would make it world class for them

- a bridge between venture finance and operating finance

### Likely complaints

- “This is too financing-centric and not operational enough.”
- “Employee option outputs do not feel fully trustworthy.”
- “There is not enough cash reality here.”

## Executives

### What they will like

- High-level scenario framing and clear structure.

### What would make it excellent for them

- executive summary outputs
- stronger memo surfaces

### What would make it world class for them

- boardroom-ready explanation with no handholding

### Likely complaints

- “I can use this interactively, but I cannot present it cleanly.”
- “The outputs are not self-explanatory outside the app.”

## Board Members

### What they will like

- Conceptually, the app addresses questions they care about.

### What would make it excellent for them

- printable, assumption-labeled outputs
- tighter trust signaling

### What would make it world class for them

- polished artifacts they can review asynchronously

### Likely complaints

- “Where is the report?”
- “What assumptions drove this?”
- “Why should I trust this exact number?”

## Complaint Resistance Diagnosis

### What makes the product good

- It reduces confusion for many users.

### What would make it excellent

- eliminating direct complaints about wrongness, ambiguity, and weak output surfaces

### What would make it world class

- leaving sophisticated users with enhancement ideas rather than credibility objections

## What Prevents Complaint Resistance Today

- trust leaks in modeled mechanics
- ambiguous capital semantics
- weak communication/export surfaces
- incomplete institutional polish
- incomplete realism in advanced venture cases
